## Survey A — categories

### Dynamical systems & chaos (ODE trajectories)  (`C01-dynsys-chaos`, ODE/dynamical-system)
- **in/out:** scalar time t (and optionally an initial-condition / parameter vector concatenated to t for a parametric trajectory net) → the state vector at that time, e.g. (theta1, theta2, p1, p2) for a double pendulum, (x,y,z) for Lorenz, or all 6N coordinates+momenta for an N-body system
- **defining:** There is NO spatial field at all. The unknown is a time-parameterized curve in a low-dimensional STATE space governed by a system of ODEs (often nonlinear, conservative, and chaotic). The PINN learns t -> state, so the 'solution' is a path that moves, not a picture that fills a 2D domain. This is the class PINN-Lab is missing entirely.
- **examples:** Double pendulum — Euler-Lagrange / Hamilton's equations for the planar two-link chain; canonical chaotic ODE, sensitive dependence on initial conditions; Lorenz '63 system — dx/dt=sigma(y-x), dy/dt=x(rho-z)-y, dz/dt=xy-beta z; the butterfly attractor, positive largest Lyapunov exponent ~0.906; Gravitational N-body / restricted 3-body — Newton's law of gravitation, Hamilton's equations with H = sum p^2/2m - sum G m_i m_j / r_ij; Van der Pol oscillator — x'' - mu(1-x^2)x' + x = 0; stiff limit cycle / relaxation oscillations; SIR/SEIR epidemic ODEs — dS/dt=-beta SI, dI/dt=beta SI - gamma I, dR/dt=gamma I (the EX02_epidemic case already in PINN-Lab belongs here, not on a heatmap)
- **PINN + SOTA:** Residual loss on each ODE component r_i = d(net_i)/dt - f_i(net, t) via autodiff in t, plus an initial-condition loss at t0. SOTA add-ons: Hamiltonian Neural Networks (Greydanus 2019) and Lagrangian NNs that learn H or L and derive the field by autodiff so energy is conserved by construction; symplectic/SympNets and neural symplectic integrators (Cai et al. 2021, arXiv:2111.15631, integrate a 3-body system 1e5 steps without drift) that split the Hamiltonian into an analytic two-body part + NN interaction; unsupervised PINNs for canonical Hamiltonian systems; time-marching / causal-weighted training and sequence-to-sequence PINNs to push the valid horizon; physics-informed Echo State Networks (Doan et al. 2019) extend the Lorenz predictability by ~2 Lyapunov times.
- **known limits:** Hard wall at the Lyapunov horizon: for any chaotic system a fixed network cannot track the true trajectory beyond ~1/lambda_max * ln(1/eps); past that only the attractor's STATISTICS (invariant measure, attractor geometry) are meaningful, not the pointwise path. Plain MLP-PINNs accumulate phase error and drift off energy on long integrations; stiff oscillators (Van der Pol at large mu) trigger the same front/stiffness pathology as stiff PDEs.
- **natural viz:** An ANIMATED object evolving in time: the actual swinging linkage of the double pendulum, orbiting point-masses for N-body, a moving dot for Lorenz — paired with a PHASE PORTRAIT (state-space trajectory, e.g. theta1 vs theta2 or the Lorenz butterfly) being traced live, plus per-component time-series plots and an energy-vs-time conservation gauge. A divergence/Lyapunov panel showing two nearby initial conditions peeling apart drives home the chaos.
- **distinct because:** It is a moving animation in state space, not a static spatial field. There is no x-y domain to color, so a viridis heatmap is meaningless here; the eye watches a trajectory and a physical mechanism move through TIME, with the phase portrait and energy gauge as the diagnostic views.

### 1D time-evolution PDEs (space-time fields)  (`C02-time-evol-1d`, time-evolution PDE (1D in space evolving in t))
- **in/out:** (x, t) — one spatial coordinate plus time → scalar field u(x,t) (or a small vector of fields)
- **defining:** The solution is a wave/front/profile in 1D space that EVOLVES, so the honest object is a full space-time field u(x,t) over an (x,t) plane, and equivalently an animated profile u(x,·) sweeping forward in time. Causality matters: the state at t depends on the state before it.
- **examples:** Burgers' equation u_t + u u_x = nu u_xx — the Raissi-Perdikaris-Karniadakis (2019) flagship benchmark; shock/steep-gradient formation; Heat / diffusion equation u_t = alpha u_xx — diffusive smoothing, the easy baseline; Allen-Cahn u_t = D u_xx - 5(u^3 - u) — stiff reaction term, sharp moving interfaces (classic PINN hard case); Korteweg-de Vries u_t + u u_x + u_xxx = 0 — solitons that travel and interact; Convection / advection u_t + c u_x = 0 — the canonical PINN FAILURE case at high wave speed c (Krishnapriyan et al. 2021)
- **PINN + SOTA:** PDE residual collocated over the (x,t) domain + IC at t0 + BCs on x, autodiff for u_t, u_x, u_xx. SOTA: causal / causality-respecting training (Wang et al.) that weights residuals to enforce time-ordering; time-marching and sequential-in-time training; self-adaptive / RAR adaptive collocation refinement near fronts; weak-form / variational PINNs (WF-PINNs) for steep gradients in Burgers; hard-constraint IC/BC via output transforms.
- **known limits:** Stiff reaction fronts (Allen-Cahn) and high-speed convection are notorious failure regimes — the vanilla PINN converges to a trivial/over-smoothed solution because it ignores temporal causality and under-resolves sharp moving gradients. Long time horizons accumulate error; shocks in inviscid Burgers need entropy-aware handling.
- **natural viz:** A 2D space-time heatmap u(x,t) IS defensible here (x and t are the two axes) AND an animated line plot of the profile u(x) marching in t, with a scrubber/play control. A characteristics overlay or front-tracking line shows where steep gradients live. The animation is the differentiator versus a frozen field.
- **distinct because:** Unlike C01 there is a genuine spatial axis, so a colored plane is legitimate — but one of its two axes is TIME, and the view should animate (profile sweeping forward) rather than freeze. It differs from C04's steady fields because nothing here is in equilibrium; the picture must move.

### 2D time-evolution PDEs (animated 2D fields)  (`C03-time-evol-2d`, time-evolution PDE (2D in space evolving in t))
- **in/out:** (x, y, t) → scalar or vector field u(x,y,t) over a 2D plane through time
- **defining:** A genuinely 2D spatial field that CHANGES every timestep — patterns spread, react, and propagate across a plane. The solution is a movie of 2D frames, not a single frame. This is where PINN-Lab's mining/pollution dispersion cases actually belong.
- **examples:** 2D advection-diffusion / contaminant transport c_t + u.grad(c) = D laplacian(c) + S — pollution plume / mine-tailings dispersion (PINN-Lab's pollution bucket); 2D heat equation with moving/transient sources T_t = alpha laplacian(T) + q(x,y,t); Reaction-diffusion (Gray-Scott, FitzHugh-Nagumo) — spots, stripes, spiral waves forming over time; 2D wave equation u_tt = c^2 laplacian(u) — ripples and interference propagating; Shallow-water / 2D Burgers — fronts moving across the plane
- **PINN + SOTA:** Residual over the (x,y,t) volume + IC frame + spatial BCs. SOTA: causal training, domain decomposition (XPINN/cPINN, FBPINN) to cover larger plates/longer times, Fourier-feature embeddings for any periodic/fine spatial structure, adaptive sampling that chases the moving source/front, hard-constraint BCs.
- **known limits:** Spectral bias bites on fine-scale reaction-diffusion patterns (high spatial wavenumbers under-resolved); cost grows with the 3D (x,y,t) collocation volume; long-horizon error accumulation; sharp plume edges / advection-dominated transport reproduce C02's convection difficulty in 2D.
- **natural viz:** An ANIMATED 2D heatmap with a transport timeline scrubber — the plume actually spreads, the spiral actually rotates — optionally with quiver/streamline overlay if a velocity field drives it, plus probe time-series at sensor points. The key is the play button and timeline, not the still frame.
- **distinct because:** It looks superficially like PINN-Lab's current static viridis heatmap, but the whole point is that it MOVES through a time dimension with a scrubber; freezing it to one final frame (today's bug) destroys exactly the information that defines the class.

### Steady elliptic / boundary-value PDEs (equilibrium fields)  (`C04-steady-elliptic`, steady elliptic/boundary-value PDE)
- **in/out:** (x, y) or (x, y, z) — spatial coordinates only, NO time → scalar potential field u(x) at equilibrium (temperature, pressure, electrostatic/gravitational potential, displacement)
- **defining:** Time-independent equilibrium: the field is whatever satisfies the PDE + boundary conditions, period. The solution is genuinely a single static field shaped by the domain geometry and BCs — this is the ONE class for which a still heatmap is the correct, honest view.
- **examples:** Laplace / Poisson laplacian(u) = f — steady heat conduction, electrostatics, potential flow; the textbook elliptic BVP; Helmholtz laplacian(u) + k^2 u = 0 — steady acoustic/EM fields at fixed frequency (high-k is a known hard case); Linear elasticity div(sigma)=0 with sigma=C:epsilon — stress/displacement in a loaded part; Steady Darcy flow div(-K grad p)=q — groundwater / reservoir pressure (mining-relevant); Screened-Poisson / steady reaction-diffusion equilibria
- **PINN + SOTA:** PDE residual on interior collocation + Dirichlet/Neumann BC losses (or hard-constrained via an output ansatz u = g + phi*net). SOTA: Fourier-feature / random-feature embeddings for high-wavenumber Helmholtz; domain decomposition (FBPINN, XPINN) for large or multi-scale domains; the energy/variational (Deep Ritz) formulation as an alternative loss; hard BC enforcement.
- **known limits:** High-wavenumber Helmholtz exposes spectral bias hard (oscillatory solutions under-resolved without Fourier features); complex/re-entrant geometries and near-singular corners are tricky; multi-scale coefficients need decomposition. But within its sweet spot this is the most reliable PINN class.
- **natural viz:** A static 2D heatmap / contour plot WITH iso-contour lines and a colorbar, optionally a 3D surface elevation of u, plus boundary-condition annotations on the domain edges. Because nothing evolves, no animation is needed — interactivity comes from dragging BC values or source locations and re-reading the field.
- **distinct because:** This is the only category where 'one static viridis field' is RIGHT — which is exactly why rendering all 19 cases this way is wrong. Its distinguishing tell is iso-contours + BC markers on an equilibrium field, and it is differentiated from the others by the ABSENCE of any time axis or moving object.

### Vector / flow fields (coupled velocity-pressure)  (`C05-vector-flow-field`, vector/flow field)
- **in/out:** (x, y[, z][, t]) for the flow domain → a VECTOR field — velocity (u,v[,w]) plus pressure p (and sometimes a stream function or vorticity)
- **defining:** The unknown is multi-component and directional, coupled through a divergence-free / continuity constraint. Direction and circulation are the physics; a single scalar color cannot represent it. Distinct from C03/C04 because the output is inherently vectorial and the natural view shows flow direction.
- **examples:** Incompressible Navier-Stokes: u_t + (u.grad)u = -grad p/rho + nu laplacian(u), div(u)=0 — lid-driven cavity, cylinder wake/vortex shedding (PINN-Lab's industrial-fluids bucket); Stokes flow (creeping) -grad p + mu laplacian(u) = 0, div(u)=0 — low-Reynolds microflow / slurry; Potential flow via stream function psi with laplacian(psi)=0 and u=curl(psi); Steady Darcy / porous-media flux q = -K grad p (vector flux through rock); Reynolds-averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS) closures for turbulent mean flow
- **PINN + SOTA:** Residuals for each momentum component + the continuity/divergence constraint (often enforced exactly via a stream-function ansatz so div(u)=0 by construction) + no-slip/inlet/outlet BCs. SOTA: stream-function output to hard-enforce incompressibility; non-dimensionalization and gradient/loss-balancing (learning-rate annealing) because the pressure-velocity scales differ; mixed/variational forms; Reynolds-number continuation for higher Re.
- **known limits:** Convection-dominated (high-Re) flow and thin boundary layers are hard (advection difficulty + spectral bias on shear layers); pressure is only determined up to a constant; turbulence is out of reach for vanilla PINNs; loss-term imbalance between momentum, continuity and BCs causes training stalls.
- **natural viz:** STREAMLINES and a quiver / arrow field showing direction, line-integral-convolution (LIC) texture, or animated tracer particles flowing through the domain, with a separate pressure heatmap and a vorticity map as companions. Velocity MAGNITUDE can be a heatmap, but the defining view is directional (arrows/streamlines), often animated.
- **distinct because:** A vector field reads as arrows/streamlines/flowing particles, not a flat color wash — direction and circulation are visible. Even when shown as a magnitude heatmap it is paired with a vorticity field and flow arrows, which no other category produces.

### Inverse problems & data assimilation (sparse data -> hidden field/parameter)  (`C06-inverse-data-assimilation`, inverse/data-assimilation)
- **in/out:** (x[, y][, t]) coordinates PLUS a set of sparse/noisy measurement points (sensor values) fused in via a data-loss term → the reconstructed full field AND inferred unknown quantities — hidden parameters (diffusivity, Reynolds number, source strength) or an entire un-measured field (e.g. pressure from velocity)
- **defining:** Some coefficient, source, boundary, or co-field is UNKNOWN and is recovered jointly with the solution from scattered data — parameters become trainable variables alongside the weights. The deliverable is a reconstruction-plus-discovery, and the honest view must show data vs reconstruction and report the inferred numbers with confidence.
- **examples:** Hidden Fluid Mechanics (Raissi, Yazdani, Karniadakis, Science 2020): infer velocity+pressure from a passive-scalar concentration field via Navier-Stokes — <1% error from sparse data; Parameter identification in unsteady Navier-Stokes (recover Reynolds number / viscosity from scattered velocity samples); Inverse Burgers / inverse diffusion: recover nu or alpha from interior observations (the Raissi 2019 'discovery' mode); Source-term inversion in advection-diffusion: locate/quantify an unknown contaminant source from downstream sensors (mining/pollution forensics); Inverse Stefan / phase-field inversion: recover material parameters or the latent free boundary from temperature data
- **PINN + SOTA:** Composite loss = PDE residual + data-misfit at sensor points; unknown parameters/fields are extra trainable variables (or a second sub-network). SOTA: classic Raissi-Perdikaris-Karniadakis (2019) inverse mode; multi-network models (one net for the field, one for the unknown source/boundary); adaptive/loss-weighting between data and physics; combine with B-PINNs (C09) when data is noisy to get error bars on the inferred parameter.
- **known limits:** Ill-posedness — multiple parameter sets can fit sparse data; identifiability degrades fast as sensors thin out or noise grows; the data/PDE loss weight is delicate (over-weight data -> overfit noise, over-weight physics -> ignore data); a deterministic PINN gives a point estimate with no uncertainty unless made Bayesian.
- **natural viz:** A SPLIT view: scattered sensor markers (the few known points) overlaid on the reconstructed continuous field, a residual/misfit map (data minus prediction), AND a prominent readout of the inferred parameter(s) converging over training (a number trending to its true value with a confidence band). The 'few dots becoming a full field' is the signature view.
- **distinct because:** It uniquely shows DATA POINTS fused with a reconstruction plus a live-converging parameter readout — there is a discovery panel (a number being learned) that no forward category has. The presence of sparse measurement markers and a misfit map distinguishes it instantly from a clean forward field.

### Operator / parametric surrogates (function -> function maps)  (`C07-operator-parametric-surrogate`, operator/parametric surrogate)
- **in/out:** an entire INPUT FUNCTION or parameter field (initial condition, source term, boundary profile, coefficient field) sampled at sensors — plus the query coordinates → the entire SOLUTION FUNCTION for that input, evaluated anywhere — i.e. a mapping between infinite-dimensional function spaces, re-usable across a whole family of problems without retraining
- **defining:** The network learns the SOLUTION OPERATOR G: input-function -> solution-function, not a single solution. Once trained it gives near-instant inference for any new input in the family, generalizing across initial/boundary conditions and coefficients. This is a different object entirely: a callable surrogate, not one field.
- **examples:** DeepONet (Lu, Jin, Karniadakis 2021): branch net encodes the input function at fixed sensors, trunk net encodes query coordinates — learns operators for ODE/PDE families; Fourier Neural Operator / FNO (Li et al. 2021): spectral convolutions, mesh-independent, the Burgers/Darcy/Navier-Stokes operator benchmarks; Physics-Informed DeepONet (PI-DeepONet): operator trained with PDE residual loss, no labeled solution data needed; Parametric Darcy flow: coefficient field K(x) -> pressure field p(x), across many permeability realizations (reservoir/mining); Antiderivative / reaction-diffusion operator families used as the canonical DeepONet demos
- **PINN + SOTA:** Train G over a DISTRIBUTION of input functions; physics-informed variants (PI-DeepONet) add the PDE residual of the predicted output as loss so little/no paired data is needed. SOTA: DeepONet (branch/trunk), FNO and its decomposed/large-scale variants (D-FNO), latent-space neural operators (real-time), hybrid FEM-guided operator learning, operator boosting for Pareto-efficient surrogates.
- **known limits:** Generalizes only within the trained input distribution — out-of-distribution inputs degrade sharply; resolution/discretization sensitivity for DeepONet (less so FNO); large training cost up front; spectral bias affects operator learning too (recent 2026 work specifically analyzes spectral bias in operator learning); accuracy per-instance can trail a bespoke PINN.
- **natural viz:** A TWO-PANEL interactive surrogate: left panel = an editable INPUT function (drag the initial condition / draw the coefficient field / move a slider over a parameter family), right panel = the solution field updating INSTANTLY as you edit, plus an error-vs-reference panel. The interaction (you change a function, the answer recomputes live) is the whole point.
- **distinct because:** It is the only category whose input is itself a function you can edit and whose value proposition is INSTANT re-solve — the natural UI is an input-function editor wired to a live-updating output, a fundamentally different interaction than viewing one fixed field. A parameter slider that morphs the whole solution is its signature.

### Eigenvalue / spectral / modal problems (eigenfunctions + eigenvalues)  (`C08-eigenvalue-spectral-modal`, eigenvalue/spectral/modal)
- **in/out:** spatial coordinate(s) (x[, y][, z]); the eigenvalue lambda/E is an extra trainable scalar (or scanned) → an eigenfunction phi(x) (wavefunction, vibration mode shape) together with its eigenvalue (energy level, natural frequency)
- **defining:** The unknown is a (function, scalar) PAIR satisfying L phi = lambda phi with a normalization constraint, and there is a DISCRETE SPECTRUM of them. You don't get one field — you get a ladder of modes, each with its own shape and number. Orthogonality between modes is intrinsic.
- **examples:** Time-independent Schrodinger H psi = E psi: infinite/finite square well, harmonic oscillator, hydrogen — quantum eigenstates (Jin et al. 2022, arXiv:2203.00451); Quantum eigenstates in disordered media — localized eigenstates (Anderson localization) via PINNs (arXiv:2305.06802); Sturm-Liouville / vibrating string & membrane: -(p u')' + q u = lambda w u — natural frequencies and mode shapes; Structural / drum modes: laplacian(phi) = -lambda phi on a domain — plate and membrane eigenmodes; Quasinormal modes of black holes / waveguide modes (arXiv:2205.08284)
- **PINN + SOTA:** Residual of (L - lambda) phi = 0 with lambda a trainable parameter, a normalization loss (||phi||=1) to avoid the trivial phi=0, and an ORTHOGONALITY loss to previously found modes so the net climbs the spectrum. SOTA: scanning mechanism that drives the net to successively higher eigenvalues; deflation/projection (Spectral Transformation Network, STNet) to exclude already-found subspaces; filter transforms to target an eigenvalue window; orthogonality regularization (Jin et al.).
- **known limits:** Collapse to the trivial zero solution or to an already-found mode without normalization+orthogonality losses; higher excited states are progressively harder (more nodes = higher wavenumber = spectral bias); degenerate / clustered eigenvalues confuse the search; the eigenvalue scan can miss states or converge to spurious ones.
- **natural viz:** A MODE SELECTOR / spectrum ladder: click eigenvalue n and see its eigenfunction phi_n (with its nodal structure / nodal lines for 2D membranes), an energy-level diagram (the E_n ladder), and probability density |psi|^2. For vibration modes, an ANIMATED standing-wave oscillation of the mode shape. Browsing the discrete spectrum is the interaction.
- **distinct because:** It produces a DISCRETE LADDER of labeled modes (an energy-level diagram + selectable eigenfunctions with visible nodes), not a single continuous field — the signature UI is 'pick mode n, watch it ring.' No other class has a spectrum to scroll through or an eigenvalue number attached to each picture.

### Uncertainty quantification / Bayesian PINNs (field + error bars)  (`C09-uq-bayesian`, UQ/Bayesian)
- **in/out:** (x[, y][, t]) coordinates plus noisy data; the network weights are treated as a DISTRIBUTION, not point values → a posterior PREDICTIVE distribution over the field — i.e. a mean field AND a per-point uncertainty (variance / credible interval), separating aleatoric (data-noise) from epistemic (model) uncertainty
- **defining:** The answer is not a single field but a DISTRIBUTION of fields. Every prediction carries a calibrated error bar, and the uncertainty itself is a field to be visualized. This is the category that answers 'how sure are we?' — essential when data is noisy or sparse and decisions ride on the result.
- **examples:** B-PINNs (Yang, Meng, Karniadakis, JCP 2021, arXiv:2003.06097): forward+inverse PDEs with noisy data, BNN prior + Hamiltonian Monte Carlo or variational inference posterior; Bayesian inverse diffusion / Burgers with large measurement noise — UQ on the inferred coefficient; Karhunen-Loeve + HMC or deep-normalizing-flow posterior variants of B-PINN for the same PDE families; Deep-ensemble / MC-dropout PINNs for epistemic uncertainty on contaminant-transport predictions; Stochastic PDEs / random coefficient fields where the solution is itself a random field
- **PINN + SOTA:** Place priors on weights; the PINN residual + data likelihood define the posterior; sample it with HMC (most accurate per the B-PINN study) or approximate with variational inference; alternatively deep ensembles or MC-dropout for cheaper epistemic UQ. SOTA: B-PINN (HMC vs VI comparison), KL-expansion priors for speed, normalizing-flow posteriors, deep-ensemble PINNs; B-PINNs avoid the overfitting that plain PINNs suffer under heavy noise.
- **known limits:** HMC is expensive and scales poorly to large nets / high dimensions; VI and MC-dropout are cheaper but can mis-calibrate (the B-PINN paper shows dropout gives poor uncertainty); the KL-prior is fast but hard to extend to high-dimensional problems; calibration is not guaranteed and must be checked.
- **natural viz:** A PAIRED view: the mean field next to an UNCERTAINTY field (std-dev / credible-interval width as its own heatmap), 1D slices with shaded confidence bands, and a calibration / coverage plot. The interaction is adding/removing or perturbing data points and watching the uncertainty band widen where data is sparse — the error bar is the star.
- **distinct because:** It shows TWO coupled fields (prediction + its uncertainty) and shaded confidence bands, never a single deterministic color map. The defining visual — uncertainty growing in data-poor regions — is something no deterministic category can display, and the band-around-the-curve view is unmistakable.

### Free-boundary / moving-interface problems (the interface IS an unknown)  (`C10-free-boundary-stefan`, free-boundary PDE)
- **in/out:** (x[, y], t) coordinates; often TWO sub-networks — one for the field, one for the unknown interface position s(t) → the field (e.g. temperature) AND the location of a moving interface/free boundary that is itself part of the solution and is not known in advance
- **defining:** The DOMAIN itself is unknown: a sharp interface separates phases/regions, moves in time, and where it goes is determined by a coupling (e.g. Stefan condition) — so the geometry must be solved for, not prescribed. A discontinuity in a derivative rides along the moving front.
- **examples:** Stefan problem (melting/solidification): u_t = alpha u_xx in each phase, with the Stefan condition L*ds/dt = -k[grad u] and u=u_melt on the interface s(t) — Wang & Perdikaris (2021) 'Deep learning of free boundary and Stefan problems'; Two-phase moving-interface Stefan problems with explicit interface tracking and the temperature-gradient jump (arXiv:2512.14010); Inverse Stefan: recover the latent free boundary or thermal parameters from sparse temperature data; Hele-Shaw / porous-media saturation fronts (mining tailings infiltration front); Phase-field / level-set evolving interfaces (Allen-Cahn as a diffuse-interface relaxation; KAN+level-set hybrids, arXiv:2601.09818)
- **PINN + SOTA:** One network for the field u and a second for the moving boundary s(t); losses = PDE residual in each phase + interface Dirichlet condition + the Stefan/flux jump condition coupling ds/dt to the gradient jump + IC. SOTA: multi-network field+interface models (Wang-Perdikaris); zero-level-set augmentation of the input so the derivative jump is captured; small-sample / improved-PINN schemes for 2D Stefan; level-set / phase-field couplings.
- **known limits:** The moving sharp front re-creates the stiff-front difficulty (steep gradient that must stay sharp while it moves); coupling two networks (field + boundary) makes training fragile and the loss landscape stiff; topology changes (merging/splitting fronts) are hard; long-time front position drifts if the Stefan condition is under-weighted.
- **natural viz:** An ANIMATED moving interface line/surface drawn ON TOP of the evolving field — you literally watch the melt front advance and the phase region grow — plus an s(t) interface-position curve and the gradient-jump highlighted across the front. The migrating boundary, not the background field, is the focus.
- **distinct because:** It uniquely renders a moving FRONT/INTERFACE as a distinct geometric object sweeping across the domain, with the solved-for boundary position s(t) plotted separately. Where C03 colors a smoothly evolving field, here a sharp line with a derivative jump is the headline, and the domain's shape is itself the answer.

## Survey D — dynamical flagship

### Flagship: Double pendulum — the chaotic-dynamics flagship for PINN-Lab (forward IVP PINN + sparse-data hybrid, with an honest Lyapunov-horizon failure mode)
- **ODEs:** Planar double pendulum from the Lagrangian L = T - V with absolute angles theta1, theta2 (from vertical), masses m1,m2, rod lengths l1,l2, gravity g. Let Delta = theta1 - theta2. The two coupled second-order nonlinear ODEs are:

(1)  (m1+m2) l1 theta1'' + m2 l2 theta2'' cos(Delta) + m2 l2 (theta2')^2 sin(Delta) + (m1+m2) g sin(theta1) = 0
(2)  m2 l2 theta2'' + m2 l1 theta1'' cos(Delta) - m2 l1 (theta1')^2 sin(Delta) + m2 g sin(theta2) = 0

Solved for the accelerations (the explicit residual form the PINN uses; den = l1 (2 m1 + m2 - m2 cos(2 Delta))):

theta1'' = [ -g (2 m1 + m2) sin(theta1) - m2 g sin(theta1 - 2 theta2) - 2 sin(Delta) m2 ( (theta2')^2 l2 + (theta1')^2 l1 cos(Delta) ) ] / den

theta2'' = [ 2 sin(Delta) ( (theta1')^2 l1 (m1+m2) + g (m1+m2) cos(theta1) + (theta2')^2 l2 m2 cos(Delta) ) ] / ( l2 (2 m1 + m2 - m2 cos(2 Delta)) )

First-order state form for collocation/RK45: state z = (theta1, w1, theta2, w2) with w_i = theta_i'; z' = (w1, theta1''(z), w2, theta2''(z)). Conserved energy (undamped): E = (1/2)(m1+m2) l1^2 w1^2 + (1/2) m2 l2^2 w2^2 + m2 l1 l2 w1 w2 cos(Delta) - (m1+m2) g l1 cos(theta1) - m2 g l2 cos(theta2) = const. Flagship constants: m1=m2=1 kg, l1=l2=1 m, g=9.81, IC theta1(0)=theta2(0)=120 deg, w1(0)=w2(0)=0 (a strongly chaotic regime). Energy E (and zero net angular impulse for this IC) are the conserved invariants the PINN should preserve and that we monitor as honesty diagnostics.
- **PINN setup:** INPUT/OUTPUT: a small MLP N_theta: t -> (theta1(t), theta2(t)), t in [0, T]. Two scalar outputs; angular velocities w_i = d theta_i/dt and accelerations theta_i'' come from automatic differentiation of the network w.r.t. t (no extra outputs, no finite differences). Tanh or SIREN(sin) activation (sin helps the oscillatory, high-frequency content — same spectral-bias remedy used in bench-wave1d here).

RESIDUAL LOSS: r1(t) = theta1''_AD - f1(theta,w),  r2(t) = theta2''_AD - f2(theta,w), where f1,f2 are the closed-form accelerations above. Physics loss = (1/Nc) sum over Nc collocation times of [ r1(t_c)^2 + r2(t_c)^2 ] (the two ODE residuals squared and averaged over collocation times, exactly as flagged). Total L = lambda_phys L_res + lambda_data L_data (+ lambda_ic L_ic if ICs are soft) + lambda_E L_energy, with an optional energy-conservation penalty L_energy = mean (E(t_c) - E0)^2 to fight numerical energy drift.

IC HANDLING — HARD vs SOFT (recommend HARD, matching the repo's output-transform pattern): 
  * HARD (preferred): output transform theta_i_hat(t) = theta_i(0) + (1 - exp(-t)) * w_i(0) ... is awkward for velocity; the clean second-order ansatz is theta_i_hat(t) = theta_i(0) + t * w_i(0) + t^2 * N_i(t). Then t=0 gives theta_i(0) (position IC) and d/dt at 0 gives w_i(0) (velocity IC) because every higher term carries t^2 -> its derivative carries t -> vanishes at 0. Both ICs satisfied by construction, no IC loss term, no IC/physics weight-balancing pathology. This mirrors bench-wave1d's t^2 x(1-x) N transform.
  * SOFT: add L_ic = sum_i [ (theta_i(0)-theta_i0)^2 + (w_i(0)-w_i0)^2 ]. Simpler but, critically, soft ICs are exactly what lets the network 'cheat' on a chaotic system (see honest_known_limits) — it trades a tiny IC violation for an easier nearby trajectory. Prefer hard.

COLLOCATION IN TIME: Nc collocation points on [0,T], uniform or RAR (residual-based adaptive refinement, a method already in the repo's dossier) so points concentrate where the residual is largest (the fast swings). Anti-aliased by scaling t to [0,1].

RECOMMENDED TRAINING (because of stiffness + chaos): vanilla full-domain Adam fails — NTK analysis (Wang/Sankaran/Perdikaris) shows PINNs implicitly minimize LATE-time residuals first, violating causality, and on a chaotic system that converges to a wrong-but-physical trajectory. Use, in order of importance:
  (1) CAUSAL / time-weighted training: weight each collocation time by w_i = exp(-epsilon * sum_{k<i} L_res(t_k)) so a later interval only gets learned once all earlier intervals are accurate; advance until all w_i exceed tolerance delta=0.99; anneal epsilon. Gives 10-100x accuracy on the chaotic Lorenz benchmark and is the single most important ingredient for the pendulum.
  (2) SEQUENTIAL TIME-MARCHING / domain decomposition: split [0,T] into short windows [t_k, t_{k+1}]; train window k, freeze, hand its end-state as the hard IC of window k+1 (a PINN analog of a time-stepper). The TU Graz result is explicit: domain decomposition + appropriate loss weighting is what stops the cheating.
  (3) CURRICULUM in horizon: start with a tiny T (near-trivial, near-linear), grow T as loss converges — never ask the optimizer to fit a long chaotic horizon cold.
  L-BFGS polish after Adam, as elsewhere in the repo.
- **data-physics hybrid:** The flagship is a DATA-PHYSICS HYBRID, not pure-forward. Provide a handful of SPARSE measured angle samples {(t_j, theta1_obs_j, theta2_obs_j)} — e.g. from the public real-double-pendulum tracked-video datasets (IBM/Asseman et al.; the chaotic-pendulum tracking sets used in 'Deep Learning with Real Data and the Chaotic Double Pendulum') or from a few RK45 points with added noise as a labelled-synthetic stand-in. Add a data-misfit term L_data = (1/Nd) sum_j [ (theta1(t_j)-theta1_obs_j)^2 + (theta2(t_j)-theta2_obs_j)^2 ] to the loss. The physics residual is enforced on MANY dense collocation times (cheap, no labels); the data term anchors the trajectory at a FEW measured instants and resolves the otherwise-unidentifiable drift. This is the canonical PINN value proposition: physics regularizes between sparse, noisy observations and the data pins the specific realization the physics alone cannot select on a chaotic attractor. Weight schedule: keep lambda_data >= lambda_phys early so the data first locks the branch, then ramp physics. Honesty note: even with data, anchoring only delays divergence past the last observation — it does not buy unlimited horizon (a chaotic system de-correlates from any finite anchor at the Lyapunov rate). When real measured points are used, label the case validated-real; when noisy RK45 points stand in, label it synthetic-illustrative.
- **honest limits:** Present the failure HONESTLY as the scientific content, do not hide it. (1) LYAPUNOV HORIZON: the double pendulum at 120 deg is chaotic with a positive largest Lyapunov exponent lambda_max (order ~ a few per second for these constants). Any trajectory error grows like exp(lambda_max t); the predictable horizon is t_pred ~ (1/lambda_max) ln(1/error0). Past it, NO method (PINN, RK45, or the real pendulum's twin) can track the specific path — the PINN is accurate early and then visibly diverges from the RK45 reference. Show that divergence on screen; it is the truth of chaos, not a bug. (2) PINNs DO NOT EXHIBIT IC SENSITIVITY / THEY 'CHEAT' (TU Graz, Steger et al., NeurIPS-2022 ML4PS 'How PINNs cheat'): with soft ICs the optimizer converges to a physically-correct solution that only MARGINALLY violates the prescribed IC but, because the system is chaotic, corresponds to a DIFFERENT trajectory than intended — it shifts the IC to an easier-to-learn nearby solution. So a 'converged, low-loss' PINN can still be the wrong trajectory. Mitigations to state: hard IC constraint, domain decomposition + loss weighting, causal training; even then the horizon limit (1) remains. (3) SPECTRAL BIAS / stiffness: PINNs learn low frequencies first and struggle with the fast late-time swings; SIREN + causal weighting help but do not remove the horizon. (4) Energy drift: an unconstrained PINN slowly violates energy conservation; we monitor E(t)-E0 and optionally penalize it, but a penalty trades residual accuracy for invariant accuracy — report both. The VALIDATION ANCHOR is a high-accuracy adaptive RK45 (and a symplectic check) integration of the same ODE/IC; the PINN is judged by where its trajectory leaves the RK45 reference within a tolerance, and that leave-time is reported as the headline honest metric.
- **viz:** Animated swinging double-pendulum LINKAGE driven by theta1(t),theta2(t): two rods from pivot -> bob1 -> bob2, positions x1=l1 sin th1, y1=-l1 cos th1, x2=x1+l2 sin th2, y2=y1-l2 cos th2; play/scrub the time slider; PINN linkage overlaid (ghosted) on the RK45 linkage so the viewer SEES them coincide early and separate at the horizon.; Traced PATH of the lower bob (x2,y2) over time — the characteristic chaotic ribbon; PINN trace vs RK45 trace in two colors, with a marker at t_pred where they diverge.; theta1-vs-theta2 phase portrait AND the (theta, theta_dot) phase portraits (w1 vs theta1, w2 vs theta2) from AD-differentiated network outputs; PINN curve over the reference orbit.; POINCARE section: sample the state every time the system crosses a chosen surface (e.g. theta1=0 with w1>0) and scatter (theta2, w2); the fractal point cloud is the chaos fingerprint — PINN-derived points vs RK45 points (they should overlap only within the predictable window).; ENERGY DRIFT over time: E(t)-E0 for the PINN (and for the RK45/symplectic reference, ~flat) — exposes the network's slow invariant violation honestly; toggling the energy penalty visibly flattens it.; BUTTERFLY-EFFECT side-by-side: two nearly identical ICs (delta=1e-3 rad) animated as twin linkages, with |delta-state|(t) plotted on a log axis showing the straight-line exp(lambda_max t) growth and an annotated Lyapunov-horizon line — the same exponential that caps the PINN's own accuracy.; Residual / causal-weight heatmap over [0,T] during training (optional 'how it converges' panel): residual magnitude vs time and the causal weights w_i sweeping from early-time to late-time, visualizing why time-marching/causal training is needed.
