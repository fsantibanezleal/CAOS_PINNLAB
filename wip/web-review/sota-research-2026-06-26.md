# SOTA + beyond-SOTA research (deep-research workflow, verified) — 2026-06-26

Source: deep-research harness (117 agents, 21 adversarially-verified claims). Transcribe into Methodology/Benchmark/Experiments; keep the HONEST LIMITs.

## Executive summary
This pass verified 21 claims spanning five PINN method families with strong primary-source support: training/weighting (causal training, RBA, NTK/gradient-norm balancing, PirateNets), architectures (SPINN, PI-KAN/cPIKAN, PirateNets), adaptive sampling (RAD/RAR-D), and domain decomposition (XPINN/cPINN, FBPINNs). The dominant SOTA pattern is that most gains come from training-time interventions (causal reweighting, residual-based attention, NTK balancing, adaptive collocation resampling) that leave a standard feed-forward MLP intact and are therefore fully compatible with an offline-train then ONNX-export then onnxruntime-web replay pipeline; the main architectural exports (SPINN's per-axis tensor product, PirateNets' gated residual MLP, Chebyshev-KAN) are likewise ONNX-expressible. The most robust honest limits confirmed here are that standard PINNs fail on chaotic/turbulent regimes (only causal training first cracked Lorenz/KS/NS, and even then only over a finite pre-Lyapunov window) and struggle on large or multi-scale domains (the explicit motivation for domain decomposition). Critically, the research question's requested POSITIVE honest-scope statement (classical FEM/FVM/spectral usually wins a single forward solve while PINNs win at inverse/sparse-data/parametric/high-dim problems) was NOT verifiable against the cited source in this pass (that specific framing was refuted 0-3), and four requested families (operator learning FNO/DeepONet, Hamiltonian/Lagrangian/symplectic dynamical-system nets, inverse problems and data assimilation, and uncertainty quantification) were reached by no surviving claim and remain open for a second research pass.

## Verified findings (10)

### Causal training (Wang, Sankaran, Perdikaris) is a simple loss reformulation that weights the PDE residual to respect temporal causality (later time steps are optimized only once earlier ones are accurate).
- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0 (3 claims merged)
- **Sources:** arXiv:2203.07404 (Wang, Sankaran, Perdikaris, 'Respecting causality is all you need for training physics-informed neural networks', CMAME 2024)
- **Claim:** Causal training (Wang, Sankaran, Perdikaris) is a simple loss reformulation that weights the PDE residual to respect temporal causality (later time steps are optimized only once earlier ones are accurate). It is the family-1 SOTA technique that first made PINNs succeed on chaotic/turbulent systems (Lorenz, Kuramoto-Sivashinsky chaotic regime, 2D Navier-Stokes turbulent regime) where standard/existing PINN formulations fail because they cannot respect the spatio-temporal causal structure of physical evolution. It changes only training-time loss weights, leaving a standard MLP, so it is fully ONNX-exportable for browser replay.
- **Evidence + HONEST LIMIT + ladder:** Merged from 3 unanimous (3-0) claims verified verbatim against the primary paper (abstract quotes for the failure diagnosis, the 'simple re-formulation... explicitly account for physical causality' fix, and the Lorenz/KS/NS SOTA + 'first time PINNs succeed' novelty). Improves over the naive uniform-weighting baseline. HONEST LIMIT to transcribe: success is over a finite pre-Lyapunov window only. Lorenz shown t in [0,20]; KS loses accuracy after t~0.8 (>10% error) from error accumulation; the 'turbulent' NS case is a modest 2D Re=100, T=1 flow; the paper itself notes long-time chaotic behavior remains difficult. Independent benchmarks (PINNacle, NeurIPS 2024) qualify the 'all you need' framing as problem-dependent. Ladder: uniform weighting (classical) -> causal weighting (SOTA) -> candidate novel: causal weighting coupled with a Lyapunov-horizon-aware rollout/stopping metric so the app never overclaims beyond the predictability window.

### Residual-Based Attention (RBA; Anagnostopoulos et al.
- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0
- **Sources:** arXiv:2307.00379 (Anagnostopoulos, Toscano, Stergiopulos, Karniadakis, 'Residual-based attention and connection to information bottleneck theory in PINNs', CMAME 2024); arXiv:2009.04544 (McClenny & Braga-Neto, 'Self-Adaptive PINNs', JCP 2022) as the adversarial baseline it improves on
- **Claim:** Residual-Based Attention (RBA; Anagnostopoulos et al., Karniadakis group) is a gradient-less, per-collocation-point weighting scheme driven by an exponential moving average of the evolving cumulative PDE residuals; it accelerates convergence at no extra computational cost and, unlike self-adaptive (SA-PINN) weighting, requires no adversarial min-max training. Training-time only, so it leaves a standard MLP and is ONNX-exportable for browser replay.
- **Evidence + HONEST LIMIT + ladder:** 3-0, every clause verbatim-confirmed in the abstract. Improves over SA-PINNs by replacing gradient-ascent/saddle-point weight updates with a cheap O(N) EMA rule lambda_i <- (1-eta)lambda_i + eta*r_i/||r||_inf. HONEST LIMIT: documented weight-saturation and late-phase non-convergence; 'no extra cost' is the authors' framing (a small O(N) update, not literally zero). IMPORTANT: a separate claim that RBA 'consistently reaches ~1e-5 rel. L2' was REFUTED 0-3, so do NOT attach a specific accuracy floor to RBA in the app. Ladder: uniform (classical) -> SA-PINN adversarial weighting (prior SOTA) -> RBA gradient-less attention (SOTA); candidate novel: combine RBA per-point weights with causal temporal weighting.

### NTK / gradient-norm loss balancing (Wang, Yu, Perdikaris) diagnoses PINN training failure as a discrepancy in the convergence rates of the different loss components (PDE residual vs boundary/data) revealed by Neural Tangent Kernel analysis, and fixes it with an algorithm that uses the NTK eigenvalues to adaptively recalibrate the per-term loss weights.
- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0 (2 claims merged)
- **Sources:** arXiv:2007.14527 (Wang, Yu, Perdikaris, 'When and why PINNs fail to train: a neural tangent kernel perspective', J. Comput. Phys. 449:110768, 2022)
- **Claim:** NTK / gradient-norm loss balancing (Wang, Yu, Perdikaris) diagnoses PINN training failure as a discrepancy in the convergence rates of the different loss components (PDE residual vs boundary/data) revealed by Neural Tangent Kernel analysis, and fixes it with an algorithm that uses the NTK eigenvalues to adaptively recalibrate the per-term loss weights. It is a training-time algorithm that leaves a standard MLP, so it is ONNX-exportable for browser replay.
- **Evidence + HONEST LIMIT + ladder:** Merged from 2 unanimous (3-0) claims, both verbatim-confirmed. This is the canonical 'why they fail' diagnosis underpinning the NTK/gradient-norm-balancing family; improves over unweighted loss by equalizing convergence rates across loss terms. HONEST LIMIT: 'THE root cause' overstates singularity. Krishnapriyan et al. (NeurIPS 2021, arXiv:2109.01050) attribute failure instead to an ill-conditioned loss landscape and show reweighting alone can be insufficient. NTK computation is expensive at training time but never touches the browser-replay path. Ladder: fixed lambda (classical) -> gradient-norm/NTK adaptive lambda (SOTA); candidate novel: cheap NTK-trace surrogates or fusing NTK balancing with RBA.

### PirateNets (Wang, Li, Chen, Perdikaris) is a physics-informed ARCHITECTURE that fixes the counter-intuitive accuracy loss of deeper MLP-based PINNs, tracing it to unsuitable MLP initialization that makes the network DERIVATIVES poorly trainable and destabilizes PDE-residual minimization.
- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0 (3 claims merged)
- **Sources:** arXiv:2402.00326 (Wang, Li, Chen, Perdikaris, 'PirateNets: Physics-informed Deep Learning with Residual Adaptive Networks', JMLR vol. 25, 2024)
- **Claim:** PirateNets (Wang, Li, Chen, Perdikaris) is a physics-informed ARCHITECTURE that fixes the counter-intuitive accuracy loss of deeper MLP-based PINNs, tracing it to unsuitable MLP initialization that makes the network DERIVATIVES poorly trainable and destabilizes PDE-residual minimization. It uses adaptive residual connections with trainable scalar gates (alpha initialized to 0) so the net starts effectively shallow and progressively deepens; it gains accuracy from depth and reaches SOTA (Allen-Cahn rel. L2 2.24e-5 vs Modified-MLP 5.37e-5). It is a deep feed-forward residual MLP (Fourier embedding + dense + tanh + scalar gate, alpha frozen at replay), hence ONNX-exportable.
- **Evidence + HONEST LIMIT + ladder:** Merged from 3 unanimous (3-0) claims, verbatim-confirmed including the full-text Allen-Cahn depth study (baseline MLP/ResNet error rises toward ~100% at 18 layers while PirateNet error keeps dropping; best case 'best result ever reported in the PINNs literature for this example'). Improves over plain and Modified-MLP PINNs, which degrade with depth. HONEST LIMIT: the SOTA numbers are the originating authors' own benchmarks (verified as accurately quoted, not independently replicated in this pass); the depth benefit is for deep nets, shallow nets see little gain. Ladder: MLP (classical) -> Modified-MLP / Fourier-feature embedding (prior SOTA) -> PirateNet gated residual (SOTA); this is the peak verified family-2 candidate architecture.

### Separable PINNs (SPINN; Cho et al.
- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0 (3 claims merged)
- **Sources:** arXiv:2306.15969 (Cho, Nam, Yang, Yun, Hong, Park, 'Separable Physics-Informed Neural Networks', NeurIPS 2023); arXiv:2410.13228 (Toscano et al., 'From PINNs to PIKANs' survey, 2024) as secondary corroboration
- **Claim:** Separable PINNs (SPINN; Cho et al., NeurIPS 2023) factorize the solution into per-axis 1D sub-networks combined by a sum of products (a low-rank/CP tensor decomposition), u = sum_j prod_i M_j(x_i), and use forward-mode automatic differentiation for the residual, enabling more than 10^7 collocation points on a single commodity GPU. Against conventional PINNs at equal collocation count they report 62x wall-clock and 1394x FLOPs speedups (up to ~60x on the 3D Helmholtz and 4D Navier-Stokes equations) with better accuracy. The per-axis MLPs plus tensor-product combination are ONNX-exportable, with the tensor product baked into the export or done in JS.
- **Evidence + HONEST LIMIT + ladder:** Merged from 3 unanimous (3-0) claims; the 62x/1394x figures are independently corroborated (NeurIPS proceedings, OpenReview id=dEySGIcDnI, ACM DL 10.5555/3666122.3667154, project page). Improves over point-wise PINNs by slashing forward-propagation count in multi-dimensional PDEs. HONEST LIMIT: the speedup depends on a separable tensor-PRODUCT GRID of collocation points (N per axis -> N^d effective points via O(N*d) passes), so it is strongest on hyper-rectangular/gridded domains and less natural on irregular geometry; the low-rank factorization can limit expressivity for some solutions. Note the '2410.13228' cited as primary in one claim is actually a survey; the true primary is 2306.15969. Ladder: point-wise MLP PINN (classical) -> SPINN factorized (SOTA for high-dim/many-collocation); candidate novel: adaptive rank or SPINN combined with domain decomposition.

### Physics-informed Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (PIKANs) are a distinct architecture family that replaces MLP layers with learnable univariate basis functions (Kolmogorov-Arnold representation); the Chebyshev variant cPIKAN uses T_n(tanh(zeta)) as inner/outer functions and CAN reach improved performance with FEWER parameters than MLP-based PINNs.
- **Confidence:** medium · **Vote:** 3-0 on the two confirmed sub-claims; MLP-parity framing refuted 0-3
- **Sources:** arXiv:2410.13228 (Toscano, Oommen, Varghese, Zou, Ahmadi Daryakenari, Wu, Karniadakis, 'From PINNs to PIKANs: Recent Advances in Physics-Informed Machine Learning', 2024); arXiv:2406.02917 (Shukla, Toscano, Wang, Zou, Karniadakis, 'A comprehensive and FAIR comparison between MLP and KAN representations...', CMAME 2024)
- **Claim:** Physics-informed Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (PIKANs) are a distinct architecture family that replaces MLP layers with learnable univariate basis functions (Kolmogorov-Arnold representation); the Chebyshev variant cPIKAN uses T_n(tanh(zeta)) as inner/outer functions and CAN reach improved performance with FEWER parameters than MLP-based PINNs. The forward pass is expressible in standard tensor ops (tanh plus a polynomial recurrence), so it is ONNX-exportable, though less battle-tested than MLP export.
- **Evidence + HONEST LIMIT + ladder:** Two constituent claims are individually 3-0 verified: (a) cPIKAN definition and the parameter-efficiency framing ('can lead to improved performance with fewer parameters', survey 2410.13228 verified in full text incl. the exact formula phi = w_n sum c_n T_n(tanh(zeta))); (b) PI-KANs 'still lack robustness as they may diverge for different random seeds or higher order orthogonal polynomials' (FAIR comparison 2406.02917). CONTESTED STATUS (why medium overall): the stronger framing that modified polynomial PI-KANs reach MLP/DeepONet PARITY was REFUTED 0-3, and 'vanilla B-spline KAN underperforms MLP' was split 1-2 (unconfirmed). cPIKAN also costs MORE GPU time per iteration and struggles on complex geometry. Net: real parameter-efficiency but unstable, NOT established SOTA-parity. Present as a candidate architecture with an explicit instability caveat, never a reliable default. Ladder: MLP (classical) -> cPIKAN (candidate/experimental).

### Residual-based adaptive sampling (Wu, Zhu, Tan, Kartha, Lu; DeepXDE group) introduces RAD (residual-based adaptive distribution: resample all collocation points from a residual-based PDF) and RAR-D (residual-based adaptive refinement with distribution: add new points drawn from that PDF), which improve PINN accuracy while using FEWER residual points than uniform/non-adaptive sampling across forward and inverse problems.
- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0 (2 claims merged)
- **Sources:** arXiv:2207.10289 (Wu, Zhu, Tan, Kartha, Lu, 'A comprehensive study of non-adaptive and residual-based adaptive sampling for physics-informed neural networks', CMAME 2023, DOI 10.1016/j.cma.2022.115671)
- **Claim:** Residual-based adaptive sampling (Wu, Zhu, Tan, Kartha, Lu; DeepXDE group) introduces RAD (residual-based adaptive distribution: resample all collocation points from a residual-based PDF) and RAR-D (residual-based adaptive refinement with distribution: add new points drawn from that PDF), which improve PINN accuracy while using FEWER residual points than uniform/non-adaptive sampling across forward and inverse problems. Sampling is a training-time strategy that leaves a standard MLP, so it is ONNX-exportable for browser replay.
- **Evidence + HONEST LIMIT + ladder:** Merged from 2 unanimous (3-0) claims, verbatim-confirmed against the abstract; this is the canonical origin paper (6000+ simulations, DeepXDE lineage). Improves over uniform/Latin-hypercube sampling. HONEST LIMIT: the win carries a 3-5x resampling/training overhead and degrades for high-dimensional and high-order PDEs; the greedy short-term bias can oversample local features (per 2024-2025 follow-ups RL-PINNs arXiv:2504.12949, R-PINN arXiv:2506.10243, RAMS). 'Fewer points' means fewer points to reach a target accuracy, not a free lunch (RAD keeps N fixed but redistributes; RAR-D adds points). Ladder: uniform sampling (classical) -> RAR (prior) -> RAD/RAR-D (SOTA); candidate novel: RL- or continuous-distribution-driven resampling.

### XPINN (Jagtap & Karniadakis) is a generalized space-time domain-decomposition framework that deploys a separate neural network in each smaller subdomain, giving larger representation and parallelization capacity than a single-network PINN; it generalizes cPINN in applicability (any PDE, not only conservation laws) and in decomposition (arbitrary cuts in space and time), which cPINN cannot do.
- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0 (2 claims merged)
- **Sources:** Jagtap & Karniadakis, 'Extended Physics-Informed Neural Networks (XPINNs): A Generalized Space-Time Domain Decomposition Based Deep Learning Framework for Nonlinear PDEs', Commun. Comput. Phys. 28(5), 2020 (global-sci.com/cicp/article/view/6911); Jagtap, Kharazmi, Karniadakis, 'Conservative PINNs (cPINN)', CMAME 364:113028, 2020
- **Claim:** XPINN (Jagtap & Karniadakis) is a generalized space-time domain-decomposition framework that deploys a separate neural network in each smaller subdomain, giving larger representation and parallelization capacity than a single-network PINN; it generalizes cPINN in applicability (any PDE, not only conservation laws) and in decomposition (arbitrary cuts in space and time), which cPINN cannot do. At replay each subdomain network is ONNX-exportable (as separate models or one graph with per-subdomain inference routing).
- **Evidence + HONEST LIMIT + ladder:** Merged from 2 unanimous (3-0) claims, verbatim-confirmed from the abstracts (large representation + parallelization capacity; 'Unlike cPINN, XPINN can be extended to any type of PDEs' and arbitrary space-time decomposition). Improves over single-network PINN via divide-and-conquer capacity and parallelism; cPINN is the conservation-law specialization using flux continuity. HONEST LIMIT to transcribe: larger capacity does NOT guarantee better generalization. Hu, Jagtap, Karniadakis, Kawaguchi, 'When Do XPINNs Improve Generalization?' (arXiv:2109.09444, SIAM J. Sci. Comput. 2022) shows XPINN can be better, similar, or WORSE than a single net depending on the per-subdomain data-vs-complexity balance; interface losses add tuning burden. 'Strictly generalizes' is loose (different interface conditions; for conservation laws cPINN's flux continuity can conserve better). Ladder: single-net PINN (classical) -> cPINN (conservation-law SOTA) -> XPINN (general SOTA).

### FBPINNs (Moseley, Markham, Nissen-Meyer) are a domain-decomposition method that expresses the solution as a sum of finite-element-inspired compact-support basis functions, LEARNED by many small networks defined over small, OVERLAPPING subdomains.
- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0 (2 claims merged)
- **Sources:** arXiv:2107.07871 (Moseley, Markham, Nissen-Meyer, 'Finite Basis Physics-Informed Neural Networks (FBPINNs): a scalable domain decomposition approach for solving differential equations', Adv. Comput. Math. 2023, DOI 10.1007/s10444-023-10065-9)
- **Claim:** FBPINNs (Moseley, Markham, Nissen-Meyer) are a domain-decomposition method that expresses the solution as a sum of finite-element-inspired compact-support basis functions, LEARNED by many small networks defined over small, OVERLAPPING subdomains. They simultaneously attack two PINN failure modes: spectral bias (via separate input normalization per subdomain) and optimization complexity that grows with domain size (via many smaller networks in a parallel divide-and-conquer scheme), outperforming standard PINNs on large and/or multi-scale domains. The sum of (fixed window function times small-net output) uses standard ops, so it is ONNX-exportable.
- **Evidence + HONEST LIMIT + ladder:** Merged from 2 unanimous (3-0) claims, verbatim-confirmed against the abstract (compact-support basis learned on small overlapping subdomains; spectral bias via per-subdomain normalization; complexity reduced via many small parallel nets; 'outperforming standard PINNs in both accuracy and computational resources'). Overlapping-Schwarz decomposition distinguishes it from XPINN/cPINN non-overlapping interfaces. HONEST LIMIT: overlapping subdomains plus window functions add hyperparameters (subdomain layout, overlap width, normalization); the scaling benefit is the paper's own claim. Ladder: single-net PINN (classical) -> XPINN/cPINN non-overlapping (SOTA) -> FBPINN overlapping finite-basis (SOTA for large/multi-scale); candidate novel: learned/adaptive subdomain layout.
