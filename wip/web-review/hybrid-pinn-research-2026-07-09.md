## Executive summary
The empirical claim is well established in peer-reviewed sources: pure physics-only ("vanilla") forward PINNs are less accurate and less efficient than high-order CFD solvers on standard forward problems (Cai, Mao, Wang, Yin, Karniadakis, Acta Mechanica Sinica 2021) and empirically break down as the regime hardens, reaching near-100% error on convection/reaction/diffusion (Krishnapriyan et al., NeurIPS 2021) and suffering convergence stagnation on complex CFD geometries with long-range dependencies such as the backward-facing step (Kumar & Ranjan 2026), whereas hybrid data+physics PINNs succeed on adverse-pressure-gradient boundary layers and separation/reattachment (periodic hill, BFS) using only sparse supervision (Hanrahan et al. 2023). The scaffold/anchoring mechanism is a documented OPTIMIZATION pathology, not a capacity limit: the PDE-residual loss is ill-conditioned and its gradients dominate (NTK eigenvalues K_rr greatly exceed K_uu), so the residual converges fast while the boundary/data term's gradients vanish and the network settles on a low-residual but wrong solution; a properly weighted data (observation) term supplies the missing gradient signal that anchors the optimization to the correct, realizable solution among the infinitely many that satisfy the PDE (Krishnapriyan 2021; Wang-Yu-Perdikaris NTK 2020; Wang-Teng-Perdikaris gradient pathologies 2020). The honest ladder is: pure-physics forward PINN (works only in benign, well-posed regimes, the weak rung), then hybrid data+physics PINN / data assimilation (the practical winner for inverse and reconstruction problems where data is REQUIRED to make the ill-posed problem solvable), then physics-informed operator learning (DeepONet, trainable even physics-only, with the PDE penalty cutting data demand); note the strong claim that hybrid PINNs beat classical CFD on accuracy AND efficiency whenever any data is available was REFUTED, so the "engineering winner" label must be scoped to the data-assimilation/inverse niche, not general forward CFD. Seminal citations: Raissi-Perdikaris-Karniadakis PINN framework (JCP 2019; forward+inverse anchor points; NS cylinder wake Re=100), Hidden Fluid Mechanics (Raissi-Yazdani-Karniadakis, Science 2020; the seminal hybrid CFD data-assimilation PINN, geometry/IC/BC-agnostic, robust to noise), the Cai et al. PINNs-for-fluids review (2021), and physics-informed DeepONet (Wang-Wang-Perdikaris, Science Advances 2021). Recommendation for PINN-Lab: add a hybrid data+physics flow-reconstruction case (sparse velocity "sensors" plus the incompressible NS or RANS residual reconstructing the full velocity/pressure field on a separation/reattachment geometry such as a backward-facing step or cylinder wake, offline-trained as a plain MLP, ONNX-exported, browser-replayed), ideally shipping a pure-physics-only baseline that visibly stalls to make the contrast concrete; and reframe the existing cases explicitly on the ladder, heat2d-inverse as the "data required to make an inverse problem solvable" rung, soil-heat-real as real-data assimilation (labeled reconstruction, not prediction from nothing), and uq-bpinn as the noise-robustness/uncertainty facet that mirrors HFM's robustness to noisy observations.

- **Evidence + sources:** Cai, Mao, Wang, Yin, Karniadakis, 'PINNs for fluid mechanics: A review' (Acta Mechanica Sinica 2021, arXiv:2105.09506, DOI 10.1007/s10409-021-01148-1) states verbatim: 'the current generation of PINNs is not as accurate or as efficient as high-order CFD codes for solving the standard forward problems,' attributing this to minimizing a 'high-dimensional non-convex' loss. Independently corroborated by Grossmann et al. 2024 ('Can PINNs beat the FEM?', arXiv:2302.04107): PINNs 'have not been able to outperform the finite element method.'

### The scaffold/anchoring mechanism is an OPTIMIZATION pathology, not a network-capacity limit: the network can represent the solution, but the PDE-residual (physics) term is ill-conditioned and its gradients dominate while the boundary/data-fitting term's gradients vanish, so the network minimizes the residual and ignores the data, returning a wrong solution.
- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0 for [10],[11],[18],[19],[21],[22]; 2-1 for the NTK re-weighting remedy [20]
- **Claim:** The scaffold/anchoring mechanism is an OPTIMIZATION pathology, not a network-capacity limit: the network can represent the solution, but the PDE-residual (physics) term is ill-conditioned and its gradients dominate while the boundary/data-fitting term's gradients vanish, so the network minimizes the residual and ignores the data, returning a wrong solution. A properly weighted data (observation) term supplies the missing gradient signal that anchors the optimization to the correct solution among the infinitely many that satisfy the PDE.
- **Evidence + sources:** Krishnapriyan et al. (NeurIPS 2021, arXiv:2109.01050): failure modes 'are not due to the lack of expressivity in the NN architecture, but ... the PINN's setup makes the loss landscape very hard to optimize'; the same paper shows 'adding/increasing the PDE-based soft constraint regularization ... makes it more complex and harder to optimize,' while lowering the physics weight eases the landscape but yields PDE-violating solutions. Wang, Yu, Perdikaris, 'When and why PINNs fail to train: an NTK perspective' (arXiv:2007.14527, JCP 2022): 'the eigenvalues of K_rr are much greater than K_uu,' so 'the PDE residual converges much faster than fitting the PDE boundary conditions, which may prevent the network from approximating the correct solution'; their remedy adaptively re-weights loss terms using NTK eigenvalues. Wang, Teng, Perdikaris, 'Understanding and mitigating gradient pathologies in PINNs' (arXiv:2001.04536, SIAM J. Sci. Comput. 2021): the model is 'heavily biased towards ... a small PDE residual, but without ... respecting the given boundary conditions,' because the boundary/data gradients are 'sharply concentrated around zero' relative to the residual gradients; the competing objectives produce 'stiff' gradient-flow dynamics. Corroborated by 2024-2026 loss-landscape work (e.g. arXiv:2402.01868).
- **Sources:** https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2021/file/df438e5206f31600e6ae4af72f2725f1-Paper.pdf ; https://arxiv.org/pdf/2007.14527 ; https://arxiv.org/pdf/2001.04536

### Identifiability / spectrum anchor: the data (observation) loss term is OPTIONAL for well-posed forward problems with known parameters and IC/BC, but is REQUIRED to make inverse / ill-posed problems (unknown PDE parameters or unknown IC/BC) solvable.
- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0 for merged claims [3],[13]
- **Claim:** Identifiability / spectrum anchor: the data (observation) loss term is OPTIONAL for well-posed forward problems with known parameters and IC/BC, but is REQUIRED to make inverse / ill-posed problems (unknown PDE parameters or unknown IC/BC) solvable. Data selects the unique realizable solution, which is why the hybrid regime is assimilation/reconstruction rather than prediction from nothing.
- **Evidence + sources:** Cai, Mao, Wang, Yin, Karniadakis review (arXiv:2105.09506 / DOI 10.1007/s10409-021-01148-1), Remark 1: for a forward problem with known parameters and IC/BC 'the data loss Ldata is not necessarily required. However, in the cases where the model parameters or the initial/boundary conditions are unknown (namely, inverse problems), the data measurements should be taken into account in order to make the optimization problem solvable.' This is the standard Hadamard characterization: inverse problems are inherently ill-posed/under-constrained, and data is what makes them identifiable. It directly grounds the honest-limits point that a hybrid PINN operates ON observations and cannot predict from physics alone in the inverse regime.
- **Sources:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10409-021-01148-1 ; https://arxiv.org/pdf/2105.09506

- **Claim:** The seminal PINN framework (Raissi, Perdikaris, Karniadakis, JCP 2019) establishes the two anchor points of the ladder, forward (data-driven solution of a PDE) and inverse (data-driven discovery of PDE parameters), with the physics prior making the network data-efficient; it is demonstrated on the incompressible Navier-Stokes cylinder wake at Re=100, reconstructing velocity/pressure and identifying unknown parameters from scattered data. - **Claim:** Hybrid data+physics PINNs succeed on genuinely complex turbulent CFD (adverse-pressure-gradient boundary layers, periodic hill with separation and reattachment, backward-facing step at turbulent Re) using only SPARSE supervision data: embedding the RANS equations as a loss regularizer makes the network robust and data-efficient, and where the PDE residual alone lacks closure (Reynolds-stress) information, sparse observations anchor the optimization to a valid solution; the network can infer the Reynolds-stress fields during training instead of using a turbulence model.

- **Confidence:** high · **Vote:** 3-0 across merged claims [4],[5]
- **Claim:** Physics-informed operator learning (DeepONet) is the operator-surrogate rung: it can learn the solution operator of parametric PDEs even physics-only (no paired input-output data, only given IC/BC plus the PDE residual as a soft penalty), and adding that PDE soft-penalty both significantly improves predictive accuracy and greatly reduces training-data demand, i.e. physics acts as a regularizer that eases data requirements at the operator level.
- **Evidence + sources:** Wang, Wang, Perdikaris, 'Learning the solution operator of parametric PDEs with physics-informed DeepONets' (Science Advances 7(40), 2021, DOI 10.1126/sciadv.abi8605, arXiv:2103.10974). Abstract: 'physics-informed DeepONets are capable of solving parametric partial differential equations (PDEs) without any paired input-output observations, except for a set of given initial or boundary conditions,' imposing physical laws 'via soft penalty constraints'; and 'this simple, yet remarkably effective extension can not only yield a significant improvement in the predictive accuracy of DeepOnets, but also greatly reduce the need for large training data-sets,' introducing 'an effective regularization mechanism.' This shows operator surrogates can be trained physics-only (a data-free operator analogue of a forward PINN) and that the physics penalty is a data-reducing regularizer. Known limitations (training difficulty, gradient pathologies, out-of-distribution generalization) refine how to optimize these models but do not dispute the capability.
- **Sources:** https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.abi8605
